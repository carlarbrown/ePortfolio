Animation

Early Humans and the Use of Fire

For this project, I transformed a image of a flame into a GIF and then created a video about how early humans used fire to survive.


For the fire image, I used both Canva and PowerPoint. I imported the fire image to a blank presentation. Then I updated the picture effect of the fire image by adding a bottom left offset shadow. I then made 2 copies of the flame so that I would have 3 flame images of different sizes. With image 1, I added blink and teeter animations to the image, changed the timing of the image to 0.5-second duration, and repeated it until the end of the slide. For image 2, I clicked Animation Painter so that the same animations from image 1 would transfer to image 2. Then, I changed the setting so that the image effect would start with the previous image and changed the delay to 0.25 seconds. For image 3, I repeated the steps I completed for image 2, but I updated the duration to 1 minute and the delay to 0.5 seconds. To create the overlay of the 3 images so it would display from the smallest image to the largest, I sent image 3 all the way to the back, and then sent image 2 backward, before I laid all three images on top of each other. I wrapped up this part of the project by testing the flame animation and then exported it as an animated GIF.


To create the entire presentation with my other images and text, I used PowerPoint. I uploaded a cave background and uploaded the flame GIF in the middle of the slide. I then added another flame image to go behind the flame GIF to fill it out and provide the campfire sticks under the flame GIF. I then found 3 images of early people that would depict my 3 topics of early using fire (warmth, food and light) and added the 3 subtitles. I then added timed transitions and animations (about 4.5 seconds each) between each image and subtitle to make each image/subtitle appear and disappear.


After I completed the PowerPoint presentation, I uploaded the mp4 to iMovie to edit the project. I found a fire-cracking mp3 and uploaded it to my video's background. Using my transcript, which included information about how fire impacted the lives of early people through warmth, food, and light, I recorded the audio for the video. After trimming the audio, and background music, I exported the file to mp4 and then uploaded it to Youtube.
